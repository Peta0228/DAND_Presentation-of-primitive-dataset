Impact of Borrowers' profile on Their Interest Rate and Loan Completion Status¶

by Peter Wu¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I will try to determine two things: first, which features are best at predicting the LoanStatus; second, which features determine the BorrowerAPR and BorrowerRate at most? Credit Score (CreditScoreRangeLower/CreditScoreRangeUpper), ProsperScore, and Term will be used primarily to determine thd three pivot variables (LoanStatus, BorrowerAPR, and BorrowerRate).

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate, current loan status, borrower income, and many others.Some points were removed from teh dataset due to thier potential influence (outliers) in visualization.

Distribution of Loan Status¶

More than half of the loans are in the Current status,and with 30 per cent already Completed loans. Nevertheless, attention should be focused on the bad loans, which are: Defaulted, Past Due, Chargedoff, and Cancelled. They are about 15 per cent of the total loans.

ProsperScore vs. Borrower Rate¶

As observed from above, ProsperScore seemed to be negatively related to the two borrowers rate. When ProsperScore was as low as 1.0, typical borrowers rate went up high to 3.0. In the middle, however, borrowers rate could take a wide range of vlaue, from 0.1 to 0.3. And when ProsperScore reached the right end extreme, few borrowers rate went up to 0.3.

Credit Score vs. LoanStatus¶

Just as expected, the CreditScore Bounds has wave-like distribution, this is most visible in the Current LoanStatus. And not suprisingly, the Defaulted, Cancelled, Chargedoff and Past Due LoanStatus, which all are bad loans, have a lower median CreditScore Bounds than the Current and InProgress LoanStatus, which are good loans (or about to be good loans). This might reveal that good debts came from a high credit score borrower on average.

LoanStatus in Different ProsperScore and Credit Score¶

One interesting fact from the plots: if a borrower has a high ProsperScore of 11.0 and a high CreditScoreRangeLower, then his/her loan is almost guaranteed to not go bad. This is witnessed in the Defaulted, Chargedoff, and PastDue boxplots, they have close to zero amount of data with high ProsperScore and high CreditScoreRangeLower at the same time.
